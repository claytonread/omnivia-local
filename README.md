# OmniVia
Omni (all) + Via (path) — "All pathways connected"

**OmniVia** is a private, local-first AI knowledge graph for businesses and advanced knowledge workers who have outgrown Obsidian and need a more structured, AI-ready way to manage organisational knowledge.

It transforms scattered documents, notes, processes, decisions, and operational context into a connected, searchable, AI-ready knowledge graph that can support business intelligence, process improvement, and future AI-assisted workflows.

---

## Project Description

OmniVia is a local-first AI knowledge management platform designed to help users move beyond static notes, folders, and manual backlinks.

It is built for people and businesses who want the privacy and control of local-first knowledge management, but need something more structured than a personal note-taking system.

OmniVia helps turn fragmented business knowledge into structured organisational intelligence by connecting documents, notes, processes, systems, people, risks, decisions, and workflows into a knowledge graph that can be searched, explored, and used as context for AI.

## Why OmniVia Exists

Many businesses already have useful knowledge, but it is scattered across local folders, Markdown notes, PDFs, Word documents, spreadsheets, process maps, meeting notes, chat exports, project documentation, decision logs, policies, procedures, and the heads of key people.

Traditional document storage can help users find files, but it rarely helps them understand how information connects.

A business may know where a document is stored, but not how that document relates to a customer process, system dependency, business risk, past decision, policy obligation, project outcome, workflow bottleneck, team responsibility, or automation opportunity.

Obsidian and similar tools are excellent for personal knowledge management, but many teams eventually need more than notes and backlinks.

OmniVia is designed for that next stage.

It aims to become a private, local-first business knowledge layer that makes organisational knowledge structured, searchable, connected, and ready for AI.

## Core Idea

The core idea behind OmniVia is simple:

> Businesses do not just need more documents.  
> They need connected, contextual, AI-ready knowledge.

OmniVia helps users create a local business knowledge graph that can answer questions like:

- What do we know about this process?
- Which documents relate to this customer workflow?
- What systems are involved in this business capability?
- What risks are connected to this process?
- What decisions have already been made?
- Where is our documentation incomplete?
- Which workflows are good candidates for AI automation?
- What context should an AI assistant use before answering or acting?
- Which people, systems, policies, and processes are connected?

## Key Features

> Note: OmniVia is in early development. Some features may be planned, experimental, or under active development.

### Local-first knowledge management

OmniVia is designed to run locally first.

This supports privacy, data ownership, offline access, local experimentation, reduced platform dependency, greater control over sensitive business knowledge, and optional integration with local AI models.

The local-first approach means users should be able to build and explore their knowledge graph without being forced into a cloud platform.

### Document and note ingestion

OmniVia is intended to ingest common business knowledge sources, including Markdown files, plain text files, PDFs, Word documents, spreadsheets, CSV files, process notes, meeting notes, project documentation, exported knowledge bases, and local Obsidian vault content.

The goal is to make it easy to turn existing local knowledge into structured, searchable intelligence.

### Obsidian vault transition path

OmniVia is especially relevant for users who have reached the limits of a local Obsidian implementation.

It is not intended to be a simple clone of Obsidian.

Instead, OmniVia is designed as the next logical step for users who want local-first control, AI-ready structure, business-focused knowledge modelling, automated entity and relationship extraction, process-aware knowledge mapping, search grounded in meaning, and better support for teams, workflows, risks, systems, and decisions.

### Knowledge graph generation

OmniVia aims to extract and connect important knowledge objects such as documents, notes, people, teams, roles, systems, processes, customers, suppliers, risks, decisions, policies, projects, metrics, tasks, dependencies, business capabilities, and automation opportunities.

Example relationships may include:

- Document mentions system
- Process depends on system
- Team owns process
- Decision affects project
- Risk relates to workflow
- Policy governs process
- Customer uses service
- System supports capability
- Task contributes to outcome

### Semantic search

Instead of relying only on keyword search, OmniVia is intended to support semantic search so users can find information based on meaning and context.

Example questions:

```text
Which documents relate to customer onboarding?
What risks are connected to the billing process?
Which systems are mentioned in our operational procedures?
What decisions have been made about the intake workflow?
Where do we have conflicting documentation?
```

### AI-ready context layer

OmniVia is designed to provide structured context for AI tools, assistants, and future agents.

This may support retrieval-augmented generation, local LLM workflows, AI-assisted summarisation, entity extraction, relationship discovery, prompt context assembly, source-grounded business Q&A, process-aware AI assistance, and future agent orchestration.

The goal is not just to store knowledge, but to make knowledge usable by AI in a controlled and traceable way.

### Business process intelligence

A key use case for OmniVia is helping businesses understand and improve their processes.

The system may support analysis of process steps, handoffs, bottlenecks, decision points, role ownership, system dependencies, risk points, manual workarounds, knowledge gaps, automation candidates, and AI readiness gaps.

### Source-grounded answers

OmniVia should aim to provide answers that are traceable back to source material.

Where possible, AI-assisted answers should show which document the answer came from, which section or chunk was used, which entities were involved, which relationships were inferred, whether the answer is directly sourced or AI-generated, and whether confidence is high, medium, or low.

## Getting Started

These instructions are placeholders and should be updated as the implementation becomes stable.

### Prerequisites

Depending on the selected implementation stack, you may need Git, Node.js, Python, Docker, a local vector database or embedded vector store, a local graph database or embedded graph engine, and access to a local LLM runtime or external LLM API.

### Clone the repository

```bash
git clone https://github.com/YOUR_USERNAME/omnivia.git
cd omnivia
```

### Install dependencies

```bash
npm install
```

```bash
pip install -r requirements.txt
```

Or, if using Poetry:

```bash
poetry install
```

### Configure environment variables

```bash
cp .env.example .env
```

Example environment variables:

```env
OMNIVIA_DATA_DIR=./data
OMNIVIA_INPUT_DIR=./data/input
OMNIVIA_VECTOR_STORE=local
OMNIVIA_GRAPH_STORE=local
OMNIVIA_LLM_PROVIDER=local
OMNIVIA_LLM_MODEL=default
```

### Add documents

Place documents into:

```text
data/input/
```

### Run ingestion

```bash
python scripts/ingest.py
```

### Run the development server

```bash
npm run dev
```

Or for a Python API:

```bash
uvicorn app.main:app --reload
```

### Open the app

Once running locally, open:

```text
http://localhost:3000
```

## Data Privacy

OmniVia is intended to be local-first.

This means the default design should avoid sending business data to external services unless the user explicitly configures that behaviour.

Privacy principles:

- Local processing should be preferred where practical
- External AI APIs should be optional
- Users should control where their data is stored
- Sensitive data should not be transmitted without clear configuration
- Local indexes and generated graph data should remain user-controlled
- Export formats should be transparent and portable
- Users should be able to delete ingested data and generated indexes

---

## Security Considerations

Security should be treated as a core design concern as the project evolves.

Important considerations include local file access permissions, sensitive document handling, API key storage, prompt injection risks, malicious document content, data leakage through external model providers, local database encryption, user permission models, auditability of AI-generated outputs, and clear separation between source content and generated interpretation.

---

## AI Safety and Trust Principles

OmniVia should avoid presenting AI-generated outputs as unquestioned truth.

Where possible, the system should:

- Link answers back to source documents
- Distinguish between extracted facts and inferred relationships
- Show confidence where appropriate
- Allow users to correct entities and relationships
- Avoid silently sending private data to external services
- Make AI behaviour configurable
- Keep humans in control of important decisions

## Development Principles

Contributors and AI coding agents should follow these principles:

- Keep the codebase simple and understandable
- Prefer clear architecture over clever shortcuts
- Write meaningful comments for complex logic
- Use plain language where possible
- Keep business logic separate from UI logic
- Keep ingestion, graph, search, and AI context concerns modular
- Write tests for core behaviours
- Avoid hard-coding provider-specific assumptions
- Make local development easy
- Document important decisions
- Keep the local-first product direction visible in all design choices

- Core behaviour has been tested
- The implementation is documented

- Sensitive data handling has been considered
- AI-generated output is traceable to source content where possible
